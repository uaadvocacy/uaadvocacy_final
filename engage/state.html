When does the 2016 legislative session begin?
Each year, the Arizona State Legislature convenes into session on the second Monday in January. Next year, Governor Doug Ducey will deliver his State of the State Address to begin the legislative session on Monday, Janaury 11, 2016.
How long does session last?
The Arizona legislature is a part-time body that typically completes their work in 100 days. Sometimes sessions can go much shorter or longer, however, depending on circumstances.
How does the UA set its legislative agenda?
After each legislative session concludes, the President of the UA meets with his cabinet to assess the outcomes of the past session and to determine what the needs of the university are in the coming year. The President works with the Arizona Board of Regents (ABOR) and the presidents of ASU and NAU to put together a higher education-wide legislative agenda. ABOR votes to approve a legislative agenda, authorizing government relations staff from the universities and ABOR to begin advocating for such policies.
Why does the UA need lobbyists?
As a public university supported by state resources, the UA has a responsibility to communicate with the Arizona legislature, the Office of the Governor and the various state agencies. The UA government relations team monitors all activity at the state government to assess any impact it may have on the university and keep the President apprised of all such developments. During the legislative session, UA lobbyists meet with members of the state House and Senate to articulate the positions of the unviersity and ABOR to ensure support and investment in higher education through legislation.
